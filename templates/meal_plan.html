Vegetable Leftover Processing & Safety

✅ Safe Processing Steps:

  1. Inspect & Sort
    • Remove all moldy, rotten portions
    • Discard fermented vegetables
    • Remove plastic, paper, foreign objects
  2. Heat Treatment (MANDATORY)
    • Boil/steam at 70°C+ for 30 minutes
    • Let cool completely before feeding
    • This kills bacteria, parasites
  3. Mixing
    • Mix 1 part vegetables : 2 parts dry fodder
    • Never feed vegetables alone
    • Maximum 3 kg/day

❌ Never Feed:

  • Spoiled/fermented vegetables
  • Onions (toxic to cattle)
  • Large amounts of raw potato
  • Oily, fried foods
  • Spicy food
  • Citrus in excess
  • Anything moldy

Weekly Processing Schedule:

Day Action
Monday Collect vegetables, sort, remove waste
Tuesday Heat-treat batch 1, cool, store
Thursday Heat-treat batch 2, cool, store
Saturday Heat-treat batch 3, cool, store

Storage: Processed vegetables last 2-3 days in cool place. Use ice if available.

Special Guidelines for Milk Production

For 10-15 Liters/Day:

  • Increase concentrate by 1 kg for every 2.5 liters above 10L
  • Feed concentrate in 2-3 portions (not all at once)
  • Ensure protein content is 16-18% of total diet

Milking Time Feeding:

  • Feed concentrate DURING milking
  • This creates positive association
  • Cow stays calm during milking

Recovery Phase (After Current Illness):

Week 1-2:

  • NO vegetable leftovers
  • Only green fodder, dry fodder, concentrate
  • Smaller portions, more frequent

Week 3-4:

  • Slowly introduce vegetables (500g, then 1kg, then 2kg)
  • Watch for any diarrhea
  • Stop immediately if problems occur

Water Requirements

  • Minimum: 60-80 liters/day
  • Lactating cow: Add 3-4 liters for every liter of milk
  • Hot weather: Increase by 20-30%
  • Change water 2-3 times daily
  • Clean water troughs daily

Signs of Good Nutrition

✅ Your cow is well-fed if:

  • Shiny coat
  • Firm, round dung (not too hard, not watery)
  • Active, alert behavior
  • Milk production steady or increasing
  • Body condition score 3-3.5 (out of 5)

❌ Warning signs:

  • Dull coat
  • Loose dung or constipation
  • Dropping milk production
  • Weight loss
  • Excessive hunger/restlessness

Cost Optimization Tips

  1. Grow Your Own Fodder
    • 1 acre CO3 grass = 20-25 cows
    • Cut cost by 60-70%
  2. Make Your Own Concentrate
    • Cheaper than commercial feed
    • Better quality control
  3. Use Vegetable Waste Wisely
    • Free feed source IF processed correctly
    • Can replace 10-15% of feed cost
  4. Seasonal Feeding
    • Store hay during dry season
    • Use silage for year-round availability

Recovery Plan After Current Illness

Days 1-3 (Immediate - With Vet Care):

  • NO solid food initially
  • Warm rice kanji (gruel) - 5-10 liters
  • Fresh water constantly
  • Follow vet's medication

Days 4-7:

  • Small amounts tender green grass (5kg, 3x daily)
  • Well-soaked hay (2kg)
  • Very light concentrate (500g)
  • Curd/yogurt (1 liter) for gut health

Days 8-14:

  • Gradually increase to normal amounts
  • Monitor dung consistency
  • If diarrhea returns, reduce feed

Week 3+:

  • Return to full feeding schedule above
  • Can introduce vegetable leftovers (heat-treated only)

Emergency Contact

  • Department of Animal Production & Health: Regional office
  • Veterinary Surgeon: Keep number handy
  • Milk Collection Center: Can often connect to vet services

Important Notes

⚠️ NEVER:

  • Feed vegetables without heat treatment
  • Give all concentrate at once
  • Let cow drink immediately after concentrate
  • Use moldy feed of any kind

✅ ALWAYS:

  • Provide clean, fresh water
  • Feed at consistent times
  • Monitor dung and milk daily
  • Maintain clean feeding area
